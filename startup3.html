The Global Blockchain Disruption: How Sky Mavis Built a Billion-Dollar Metaverse with Axie Infinity

10:21:08 AM | 11/4/2025

From a small Vietnamese startup to a global GameFi pioneer, Sky Mavis defined the "Play-to-Earn" model, turning millions of players into digital asset owners and placing Vietnam at the center of the Web3 revolution.


While many Vietnamese tech companies focused on traditional internet services, one startup looked to the future of ownership and gaming, launching a phenomenon that would reshape the global video game landscape. Sky Mavis, the visionary developer behind the blockchain game Axie Infinity, became one of the fastest-growing technology companies in the world, proving the immense potential of the decentralized economy.


The Architects of the Metaverse Economy


The success of Sky Mavis is credited to its founding team, particularly Trung Nguyen, the CEO and Co-Founder, whose belief in decentralized systems drove the company‚Äôs direction. Other core founders include Aleksander Larsen (COO) and Jeffrey Zirlin ("Jiho," Head of Growth), who brought international expertise and evangelism to the project. Their vision was simple yet revolutionary: shift power and wealth from the game developers to the players themselves.


Axie Infinity is not just a game; it is a digital nation built on the blockchain, offering players true ownership over their in-game assets‚Äîthe adorable, creature-like NFTs known as Axies.


Phase 1: The 'Play-to-Earn' (P2E) Revolution


When Axie Infinity launched, the concept of a game where digital items were owned as NFTs was nascent. Sky Mavis‚Äôs masterstroke was introducing the Play-to-Earn (P2E) model.


The Economic Engine: P2E allowed players to earn tokenized rewards, primarily Smooth Love Potion (SLP) and the governance token Axie Infinity Shards (AXS), simply by playing the game. These tokens could then be traded on external cryptocurrency exchanges for real-world money.


This model was transformative, particularly in developing economies (like the Philippines, Indonesia, and, of course, Vietnam) where the income earned from playing Axie could often surpass local minimum wages. The game offered not just entertainment, but a viable new source of livelihood, leading to explosive adoption.


The "Scholarship" Model: P2E's virality was further fueled by the "scholarship" system. Asset owners (Managers) could lend their Axie NFTs to new players (Scholars) who couldn't afford the initial investment, splitting the in-game earnings. This created an organic, self-sustaining financial network that dramatically accelerated the game‚Äôs user base and economy.


Phase 2: Building the Infrastructure (The Ronin Network)


As Axie Infinity grew, it encountered a critical bottleneck: the high transaction fees and slow speeds of the Ethereum blockchain, where it was originally built. Sky Mavis recognized that to scale a true digital economy, they needed their own dedicated infrastructure.


The Ronin Solution: The team engineered and launched the Ronin Network, an Ethereum sidechain specifically designed for blockchain gaming.


  • Zero Fees, Instant Speed: Ronin offered near-instant transaction speeds and drastically reduced gas fees, making the frequent small transactions necessary for P2E (like breeding Axies or claiming SLP) economically viable for millions of daily active users.
  • Mass Adoption Enabler: By solving the scaling problem, Ronin proved that a blockchain game could handle the demands of a global, high-volume consumer product, a feat previously considered impossible in the Web3 space. This move not only stabilized Axie Infinity but positioned Sky Mavis as a leading blockchain infrastructure provider in the GameFi sector.

The Global Impact


Sky Mavis‚Äôs journey is a powerful case study in global disruption. By creating a fully player-owned economy, they transformed the relationship between developers and users.


The company achieved a valuation of over $3 billion at its peak and, despite later market volatility and a major security breach (the Ronin bridge attack, which was managed and largely remediated), its core product remains a landmark in Web3 history.


Sky Mavis didn't just build a successful video game; it catalyzed the GameFi movement, inspiring thousands of subsequent P2E projects globally and firmly placing Vietnam on the map as a powerhouse for innovation in the decentralized digital future.
